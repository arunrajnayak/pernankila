About Pernankila

Pernankila is a village near Hiriadka, located in the district of Udupi in Karnataka. This village is blessed by divine blessings and is considered to be filled with divine power. The divine abode of Lord Shri Mahalingeshwara and Shri Mahaganapathi is situated in here which is about 20 km southeast of Udupi. The Ganapathi here is called as Udbava Ganapati as it is believed to have come from earth by itself. People believe that he removes obstacles from one's life.

History

It is believed that in Pernankila, the Lord Mahaganapathi is a Udbhava deity, who is also known as Swayambhoo (self incarnation). The divine idol of Mahaganapathi here was said to have been found by a farmer called Perna while ploughing his farm. The idol of Mahaganapathi hidden under the earth accidentally got stuck to his plough. As a result, the head portion of the idol was torn apart and astonishingly blood was gushing out of it. The farmer was awe stuck on seeing this unusual scene.

Severely perturbed Perna went to his home that day and saw an unusual dream that night. In his dream, Perna was ordained to keep upside down two Koppariges (jar that contains hidden treasure), one at the place where he found the Mahaganapathi Idol in his farm and the other near the Shivaalaya (the Shiva temple) and visit the two spots next morning.

Perna did according to the divine order which he received in his dream. To his surprise, when he visited the spots next day, he saw the place absolutely vacant where he originally found the Mahaganapathi idol, but located the same near the Shivaalaya under the Kopparige which he had placed upside down. Later, the idol was found in a nearby well and its upper portion was only visible. From that day, the village came to be known as Perna+Ankila (the plough), Pernankila. It is widely believed that with the blessings of Lord Mahaganapathi, the farmer Perna's name was immortalized and the village itself came to be identified with his name and the plough, i.e., Pernankila.

About Temple

The village is famous for Lord Shri Mahalingeshwara and Shri Mahaganapathi temple. The annual fair of the temple is celebrated with great charm and devotion. And on this day chariot of the temple is pulled by many devotees with statues of god placed in it. Ganesh Chaturthi is the holiest day in this village. Thousands of devotees visit this temple on that day and offer their prayers. Devotees firmly believe that Lord Mahaganapathi bless them and fulfills their wishes.

Special poojas and celebrations are held during the Ganesh Chaturthi during which nearly 5000 devotees visit the temple and participate in (annasantarpane), that is served on that day. Besides religious functions, cultural events are also organised on the Ganesh Chaturthi day in the temple. On the day of Ekadashi the meals are not served, the people maintain fast and offer prayers to god. During Meena in the month of March–April, on the full moon day the festivities start with the hoisting of the temple flag and Rathotsava is also organised on that day. On Tuesdays and Sankashti, more number of devotees visits the temple.

In December month, daily bhajana is conducted by Pernankila Bhajana Mandali at each houses in the village. At the end, on last saturday of December Bhajana Mangala is held in temple. During this, many other bhajana groups comes to the event and performs. This program starts at 6pm and ends at next morning 6am.

It is believed that the Pernankila Shri Mahaganapati is fond of appams prepared out of rice, coconut, ghee and jaggery. On the Ganesh Chaturthi day nearly one quintal rice is used to prepare one lac appams and distribute among the devotees. The kopparige appa is seva provided, where devotees offer prayers to god and annasantharpane is done.

Lifestyle

Pernankila is a village with around 450 families residing. As per Census 2011, Pernankila village has population of 1841 of which 915 are males while 926 are females. Rajapoori Konkani, Tulu and Kannada are the most widely spoken language in Pernankila.

In Pernankila village population of children with age 0-6 is 158 which makes up 8.58 % of total population of village. Average Sex Ratio of Pernankila village is 1012 which is higher than Karnataka state average of 973. Child Sex Ratio for the Pernankila as per census is 927, lower than Karnataka average of 948, which is a matter of concern.Schedule Caste (SC) constitutes 7.60 % while Schedule Tribe (ST) were 7.50 % of total population in Pernankila village.

There is a primary health care center at a distance of nearly 1 km from Pernankila temple. There are two shops near Pernankila temple which offer the items required for Pooja's. Wagle Stores is popular grocery shop near government primary school, where people get items required for daily use.

Agriculture is main occupation for many people here. Few people travel to Manipal or Udupi for job. There is a cashew factory where many people go to work as well.

Education

Pernankila village has higher literacy rate compared to Karnataka. In 2011, literacy rate of Pernankila village was 82.35 % compared to 75.36 % of Karnataka. In Pernankila Male literacy stands at 88.36 % while female literacy rate was 76.47 %.

There is an anganavadi kendra and a government higher primary school at the center of the village. For high school education, students needs to go to Sri Vishnumoorthy High School, Kodibettu which is around 2.5km from Pernankila. To get pre-university and higher education, students travel to Hiriadka, Moodubelle, Manipal or Udupi. Nearest engineering colleges are Nitte and Bantakal.

Weather

Pernankila usually has a pleasant climate. The climate in Pernankila is hot in summers and pretty good in winter. The village is just about 25m above the sea level. During summer season the temperature reaches up to 35°C and in winters it ranges between 20°C and 32°C. The average annual temperature is 27.0 °C. The Monsoon period is from June to October with average rain of 450 cm. In July, the precipitation reaches its peak, with an average of 160 cm rain.The Western Ghats have great positive impact on the rainfall.
